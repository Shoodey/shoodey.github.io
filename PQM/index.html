IT Project Failure

DMV Case

Story

California state spent the 1990s attempting to computerize their DMV Department of Motor Vehicules

  • Started in 1987.
  • Over 5 years.
  • $27M plan.
  • 31M drivers' licences.
  • 38M vehicule registrations.

What went wrong

Solocited a bid from one company.

CA state ended up buying Tandem hardware as well.

6 computers at a cost of $11.9M

The new system was slower than the one it was designed to replace.

Abandonned in 1994.

Repercussions

An unworkable system that could not be fixed without the expenditure of millions more.

San Fransico Chronicle - Feb 1994

A failed $44M computer project.

the Chronicle - Mai 1994

The project cost peaked at $49M, costing money even after being shut down.

Moral

No project should rely on a unique supplier.

Projection should have been made to fully estimate the project cost, faisability & performance.
